Talk Outline
📅 Friday, April 17, 2026  ·  11:00 AM – 12:00 PM
Research Auditorium R3381/3383
Co-sponsored by NIH NHLBI Data Coordination Center in partnership with the BMI division
Title: Beyond LLMs: Agentic AI for Biomedicine
  • Defines agentic AI systems: LLMs as decision-making cores that plan, use tools, maintain memory, and refine outputs through structured workflows.
  • Explains how tool use and iterative orchestration enable multi-step reasoning and coordination of complex computational tasks.
  • Covers Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and agentic RAG to improve factual accuracy by grounding outputs in biomedical literature, guidelines, and databases.
  • Discusses Reinforcement Learning (RL) for tool-augmented, long-horizon decision making, including preference optimization using comparative feedback.
  • Concludes with biomedical applications — literature synthesis, hypothesis generation, experimental planning, multimodal analysis — and responsible deployment considerations.
Pre-Reading List
Aman Chadha

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

1
Building a Hybrid Retrieval (Lexical + Semantic) RAG-based LLM System

Covers how hybrid retrieval systems combine exact keyword matching with semantic understanding to improve search reliability and reduce blind spots of either approach alone.

2
Seven Failure Points When Engineering a Retrieval Augmented Generation System

Shows that RAG systems have inherent reliability limits, with effectiveness emerging through real-world use rather than being fully designed upfront.

Aman Chadha

Knowledge Graphs + RAG

1
ClaimVer: Explainable Claim-Level Verification and Evidence Attribution of Text Through Knowledge Graphs

Uses Knowledge Graphs for fine-grained claim-level validation with clear evidence, explanations, and attribution instead of simple overall labels.

2
SKETCH: Structured Knowledge Enhanced Text Comprehension for Holistic Retrieval

Improves RAG systems by combining semantic retrieval with knowledge graphs, leading to more accurate and contextually relevant results across multiple datasets.

Aman Chadha

Agentic AI

1
Towards a Science of Scaling Agent Systems

A quantitative study showing that adding more LLM agents only helps in certain tasks, with performance governed by trade-offs between coordination overhead, task structure, and error amplification.

2
Anthropic: How We Built Our Multi-Agent Research System

A walkthrough of building agentic systems that carry out deep research by orchestrating multiple specialized LLM agents in parallel, decomposing tasks and iteratively synthesizing results.

Vinija Jain

KG / Ontologic Approaches to Knowledge Representation

1
Translating Embeddings for Modeling Multi-relational Data (TransE)

A foundational method for knowledge graph embeddings that models relations as vector translations, enabling scalable representation and inference over large structured knowledge bases.

2
A Three-Way Tensor Factorization for Relational Learning

An early and highly cited approach that factorizes relational data into latent representations, capturing global interactions across entities and forming a basis for modern KG learning methods.

Vinija Jain

LLM-based Methods for KG Building

1
A Survey on Augmenting Knowledge Graphs with Large Language Models

A comprehensive overview of how LLMs can support knowledge graph construction through extraction, schema alignment, and completion, while discussing limitations like hallucination and structural inconsistency.

2
Docs2KG: Unified Knowledge Graph Construction from Documents with LLMs

Presents a unified pipeline where LLMs transform unstructured documents into structured knowledge graphs, demonstrating strong performance in automated entity and relation extraction.

Vinija Jain

LLM-based Reasoning Methods that Utilize KGs

1
KG-GPT: A General Framework for Reasoning on Knowledge Graphs Using Large Language Models

Introduces a framework that integrates LLMs with knowledge graph traversal and retrieval, improving multi-hop reasoning and factual grounding in complex query answering.

2
Complex Logical Reasoning over Knowledge Graphs using Large Language Models (LARK)

Proposes a hybrid system combining symbolic query decomposition with LLM reasoning, enabling more accurate handling of complex logical queries over structured knowledge graphs.
