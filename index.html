  • Defines agentic AI systems: LLMs as decision-making cores that plan, use tools, maintain memory, and refine outputs through structured workflows (planning, reflection, tool use).
  • Explains how tool use and iterative orchestration enable multi-step reasoning and coordination of complex computational tasks.
  • Covers Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and agentic RAG to improve factual accuracy by grounding outputs in biomedical literature, guidelines, and databases via dynamic retrieval and iterative reasoning.
  • Discusses Reinforcement Learning (RL) for tool-augmented, long-horizon decision making, including preference optimization using comparative feedback.
  • Concludes with biomedical applications (literature synthesis, hypothesis generation, experimental planning, multimodal analysis) and the reliability, evaluation, and responsible deployment questions they raise.
Pre-Reading List

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

1
Building a Hybrid Retrieval (Lexical + Semantic) RAG-based LLM System

A primer on building hybrid retrieval systems that combine exact keyword matching with semantic search, improving reliability by reducing the blind spots of either approach used alone.

2
Seven Failure Points When Engineering a Retrieval Augmented Generation System

Identifies seven concrete points where RAG pipelines break down in practice — spanning indexing, retrieval, and generation — based on real-world deployments, with guidance for building more robust systems.

Knowledge Graphs + RAG

1
ClaimVer: Explainable Claim-Level Verification and Evidence Attribution of Text Through Knowledge Graphs

Uses knowledge graphs to perform fine-grained, claim-level fact verification with explicit evidence attribution — providing transparency into which specific claims are supported or contradicted, rather than producing a single overall label.

2
SKETCH: Structured Knowledge Enhanced Text Comprehension for Holistic Retrieval

Enhances RAG by integrating semantic text retrieval with knowledge graphs, merging structured and unstructured data for more holistic comprehension. Evaluated across four datasets (QuALITY, QASPER, NarrativeQA, Italian Cuisine), SKETCH consistently outperforms baselines on answer relevancy, faithfulness, and context precision.

Agentic AI

1
Towards a Science of Scaling Agent Systems

A quantitative study showing that adding more LLM agents only improves performance in certain tasks. Results are governed by trade-offs between coordination overhead, task structure, and error amplification — not simply by scale.

2
Anthropic: How We Built Our Multi-Agent Research System

A walkthrough of building agentic systems that conduct deep research by orchestrating multiple specialized LLM agents in parallel — covering task decomposition, iterative synthesis, and the engineering challenges of keeping such systems reliable and cost-effective.

KG / Ontologic Approaches to Knowledge Representation

1
Translating Embeddings for Modeling Multi-relational Data (TransE)

A foundational KG embedding method that models relations as vector translations between entity embeddings, enabling scalable representation and inference over large structured knowledge bases.

2
A Three-Way Tensor Factorization for Relational Learning (RESCAL)

An early and highly cited approach that factorizes relational data using a three-way tensor, capturing latent global interactions between entities and laying the groundwork for modern KG representation learning.

LLM-based Methods for KG Construction

1
A Survey on Augmenting Knowledge Graphs with Large Language Models

A comprehensive overview of how LLMs support KG construction through entity extraction, schema alignment, and graph completion, with an honest discussion of limitations including hallucination and structural inconsistency.

2
Docs2KG: Unified Knowledge Graph Construction from Heterogeneous Documents with LLMs

Presents a unified pipeline where LLMs process unstructured documents — including text, tables, and figures — into structured knowledge graphs, with strong performance on automated entity and relation extraction across diverse document types.

LLM-based Reasoning over Knowledge Graphs

1
KG-GPT: A General Framework for Reasoning on Knowledge Graphs Using Large Language Models

Integrates LLMs with knowledge graph traversal and retrieval, decomposing complex queries into sub-problems and grounding answers in structured KG facts to improve multi-hop reasoning accuracy.

2
Complex Logical Reasoning over Knowledge Graphs using Large Language Models (LARK)

Combines symbolic query decomposition with LLM reasoning to enable accurate and interpretable handling of complex logical queries — including multi-hop and conjunctive queries — over structured knowledge graphs.
